Young Adult

Young Adult

Young Adult (YA) books are typically written for a teenage audience but have gained popularity among adults too. They often feature coming-of-age themes, relationships, and personal struggles.

  • "The Fault in Our Stars" by John Green
  • "The Hunger Games" by Suzanne Collins
  • "Looking for Alaska" by John Green

Feminist Classics

Feminist Classics

Feminist classics are books that have had a significant impact on feminist literature, addressing themes of women's rights, gender equality, and social justice.

  • "The Second Sex" by Simone de Beauvoir
  • "The Feminine Mystique" by Betty Friedan
  • "A Room of One's Own" by Virginia Woolf

Psychological Thrillers

Psychological Thrillers

Psychological thrillers focus on the unstable or delusional psychological states of characters. These books often involve intense suspense, mind games, and plot twists.

  • "Gone Girl" by Gillian Flynn
  • "The Girl on the Train" by Paula Hawkins
  • "Shutter Island" by Dennis Lehane

Murder Mystery

Murder Mystery

Murder mystery books revolve around a crime, usually a murder, and the investigation to uncover the killer. These books often feature detectives or amateur sleuths solving the crime.

  • "The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo" by Stieg Larsson
  • "Murder on the Orient Express" by Agatha Christie
  • "The Silent Patient" by Alex Michaelides
